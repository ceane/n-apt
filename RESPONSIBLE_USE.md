# Responsible Use Policy

This Responsible Use Policy is **incorporated by reference into the Hippocratic License 3.0 (Version 3.0, October 2021)**. All licensees must comply with these terms as a **binding extension of the Ethical Standards** (Section 3).  

Violations of this policy constitute a breach of the License and trigger the **Duty of Care** and **termination provisions** of the License.

---

## 1. Prohibited Uses

Licensee **may not use the Software or any derivatives** for purposes that:

1. **Violate human rights**  
   - Arbitrary killing, slavery, forced labor, torture, inhumane treatment.  
   - Discrimination or denial of fundamental rights.  

2. **Engage in unlawful surveillance or privacy violations**  
   - Mass surveillance, unauthorized data collection, stalking, eavesdropping, or tracking.  

3. **Cause psychological, emotional, or physical harm**  
   - Manipulation, coercion, harassment, cyberbullying, intimidation, or emotional abuse.  
   - Licensee must **avoid use that imposes extreme, disproportionate, or sustained harm, or exploits individuals or systems in ways beyond legitimate purposes**.

4. **Use in offensive military or weaponized applications**  
   - Direct or indirect development, deployment, or enabling of harmful weapons.  

5. **Circumvent legal or ethical protections**  
   - Exploiting loopholes to bypass privacy laws, consent requirements, or regulatory safeguards.  

6. **Engage in hate crimes, unlawful discrimination, or harassment**  
   - Target individuals or groups based on **identity, belief, sexual orientation, gender, race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, ability, or any protected status**.  
   - Licensee must not use the Software to **arbitrarily single out, coerce, or manipulate** any person or group for discriminatory purposes.

## 2. Guidance for Responsible Use

Licensees are expected to:

- Act in accordance with **international human rights principles** and the ethical standards defined in the License.  
- Consider the **intent and foreseeable impact** of their use on individuals, communities, and the environment.  
- Seek legal or ethical guidance when in doubt about novel or complex applications of the Software.  
- Document measures taken to **prevent harm** and ensure transparency.

## 5. Tailored Edge Case Scenarios

The following scenarios illustrate **potential ethical dilemmas specific to this repository**. They are intended as binding examples under the Responsible Use Policy. Failure to follow the intended outcome constitutes a violation of the Hippocratic License 3.0 Ethical Standards.

---

### Scenario 1 — Direct or Indirect Harm to the United States

**Situation:** A Licensee uses the Software, models, or algorithms in a manner that could directly or indirectly affect U.S. government systems, national infrastructure, or operations critical to public safety.  

**Issue:** Even if the Software itself is unclassified, misuse could unintentionally compromise U.S. systems, violate laws, or create risks to security, privacy, or societal well-being.  

**Resolution:**  
1. Licensee **must not deploy the Software in any context that could impact U.S. federal, state, or local systems** without proper authorization.  
2. Licensee must **assess indirect risks** to U.S. systems and take proactive steps to mitigate harm, including restricting access, auditing outputs, and preventing unintended effects.  
3. Ambiguous or high-risk scenarios **must be escalated to Licensor guidance**, and any decisions issued become binding precedent under this Responsible Use Policy.  

**Rationale:** This policy ensures the Responsible Use Policy aligns with the Hippocratic License 3.0 principles, preventing harm to individuals, communities, and critical national systems, including those of the United States.

---

### Scenario 2 — Loophole (Legal but Immoral)

**Situation:** A user captures and analyzes I/Q radio signals for research purposes, then shares cognitive data or inferred behavioral patterns of individuals without consent.  

**Issue:** The technical data use does not explicitly violate copyright or patent law, but it could expose individuals to harm.  

**Resolution:** Any derivation of cognitive, personal or behavioral data or insights must comply with Responsible Use prohibitions; informed consent or anonymization is required before sharing.

---

### Scenario 3 — Overreach (Illegal but Moral)

**Situation:** A developer uses repository algorithms to prevent imminent cyber-physical (neurological or otherwise) harm, temporarily bypassing standard security or access controls.  

**Issue:** Normally, circumventing system protections would violate usage rules.  

**Resolution:** A **Good Samaritan exception** allows limited, documented intervention when immediate harm would otherwise occur, with actions logged and access revoked once the threat is mitigated.

---

### Scenario 4 — Escalated Dilemma

**Situation:** Two modules of the repository could be combined to expose a serious vulnerability in a widely used system, but doing so could also unintentionally reveal sensitive infrastructure data.  

**Issue:** Ethical principles of public safety conflict with responsible data handling.  

**Resolution:** Only report the vulnerability through secure channels that protect sensitive data; do not release exploitable details publicly. Escalation to Licensor guidance may be required if the situation is novel.

---

### Scenario 5 — Indirect or Supply Chain Harm

**Situation:** Licensee distributes models trained on data generated using ethically questionable sources. Downstream users could inadvertently violate human rights.  

**Issue:** Harm occurs indirectly.  

**Resolution:** Licensees must **audit training data, document provenance, and prevent misuse**, ensuring downstream recipients cannot exploit data or models unethically.

---

### Scenario 6 — AI/ML Behavioral Manipulation

**Situation:** A model or algorithm is capable of influencing user behavior (e.g., suggestion systems, cognitive targeting) in ways that could coerce or manipulate individuals.  

**Issue:** Directly conflicts with Responsible Use prohibitions on psychological, emotional, or physical harm.  

**Resolution:** Deploy safeguards and monitoring; automated outputs must not be used to manipulate, coerce, or otherwise harm individuals. Licensee must intervene if misuse is detected.

---

### Scenario 7 — Misuse in Pseudo-Scientific, Bigoted, or Unlicensed Medical Applications

**Situation:** A Licensee uses the Software, models, or algorithms to support research, insights, diagnostics, or operations that are:  
- Pseudo-scientific or not evidence-based,  
- Discriminatory (targeting individuals or groups based on race, gender, ability, etc.), or  
- Conducted without proper medical licensing, regulatory approval, or ethical oversight.  

**Issue:** Even if the underlying science or algorithms are technically accurate, deployment in these contexts could cause **harm, discrimination, or legal violations**.  

**Resolution:**  
1. The Software **must not be used for research, treatment, or operations that lack proper scientific validation, ethical review, or regulatory authorization**.  
2. Licensee **must implement safeguards** to prevent the Software from being applied in discriminatory, criminal, or unlicensed medical contexts.  
3. Ambiguous cases **must be escalated to Licensor guidance**, with binding precedent established to prevent future misuse.  

**Rationale:** This ensures the Responsible Use Policy protects individuals and communities from **harm caused by pseudo-science, bigotry, or unlicensed medical activities**, while maintaining alignment with Hippocratic License 3.0 Ethical Standards.
